Delicious Ekwang

Ekwang is a delicious delicacy made from grated fresh coco-yams wrapped in fresh green leaves still from coco-yams but not all coco-yams leaves, given that some of these leaves itch if eaten

This is cooked with an amazing combination of spices that leaves it with an ever desiring taste and aroma

Ingredients

Below is a list of ingredients needed to make this delicacy

  • Coco-yams
  • Fresh green coco-yam leaves
  • Palm oil
  • Smoked fish
  • Crayfish
  • Salt
  • Contry Onion
  • Onions
  • White-pepper
  • Maggi
  • Black-pepper

Preparation - steps

Steps to follow im order to make this delicious dish

  1. Step one
    • Slice beef into mouth-size pieces, season with salt and seasoning powder
    • Boil on high heat until tender with about 3 cups of water. Save Beef broth.
  2. Step two
    • Peel cocoyams with a knife or vegetable peeler and wash with water till clean.
    • Cocoyams can be itchy, so wash with gloves if you cannot handle the itch.
  3. Step three
    • Using a grater, grate the cocoyams or if using a food processor, process until puree.
    • Add 1 tsp of salt to pureed cocoyams and set aside.
  4. Step four
    • Wash the fresh green leaves thoroughly
    • Cut them into small pieces to prepare for wrapping with the coco-yam paste
    • Use part of the red oil and grease the inside of a cleaned pot
  5. Step five
    • Using a tablespoon scope portions of the paste, place on the cut leaves and wrap carefully and firmly
    • carefully arrange the wraps in the pot in a circular fashion
    • You want to carefully do this because we generally do not stir while cooking else everything breaks into a paste
  6. Step six
    • Heat water in a separate pot .
    • Add seasoning: maggi, salt, crayfish, pepper to the wrapped and carefully placed paste.
    • Leave the oil for later
    • Place the pot on on low heat for about 5 minutes. This allows the wraps to tighten
  7. Step seven
    • Avoid stirring with a cooking spoon at this point, now add beef broth with 4 cups of boiled water
    • Holding the pot by the handles, gently swirl in circular manner to enable mixing
    • You want to avoid any form of stirring
    • Let this cook for about 30 minutes
  8. Step eight
    • After 30 minutes, you can stir gently, then add palm oil and more water, let it cook again for 30 minutes.
    • Make sure you check the pot every 5 minutes to add water if need be so it doesn't burn.
    • Avoid over stirring ekwang like it becomes mushy. Also adjust taste with salt, seasoning powder or water.
    • Food is ready. Enjoy!
